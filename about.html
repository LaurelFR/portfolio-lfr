Hi, I'm Laurel Rubel

Front-end developer

When I found myself describing my latest piece of code as “elegant,” I knew I was hooked.

For a long time, my passion was psychology. It was my dream to become a therapist and help people every day. After I got my BA in Psychology, I was on track to achieve my goals. I started graduate school in counseling psychology and then… I gradually realized that being a therapist wasn’t for me. I started researching different career paths and eventually I stumbled upon a JavaScript tutorial.

Coding was fascinating. Gripping. I was eager to learn as much as I could. I’ve run into my share of frustrating problems, but for me, coding is like an endless puzzle I am determined to solve - and I have always loved puzzles. Coding is engaging and creative, and I can’t stop learning more. I have had to force myself to slow down and not get carried away trying to learn too much at once. Front-end web development plays to all my strengths and passions. It involves attention to detail, creativity, problem solving, patience and communication.

I still love psychology - and I will bring my knowledge of the mind and behavior into my career as a Front-end web developer. Coding involves a lot of communication - from my code, to the computer, to the user, and while working with other developers. I also love making my websites responsive and accessible to all. I consider user expectations and experience when creating a website, keeping in mind users with disabilities and those who are using screen readers.

My current skills include JavaScript, Bootstrap, HTML, CSS, React.js, VS Code, CLI, and Git/GitHub. I am working hard to learn more and strengthen these skills. I am currently learning advanced React.js, and intend to start learning more programming languages, frameworks, and principles including Node.js, Next.js, and TypeScript.
